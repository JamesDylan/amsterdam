03 Differentiation · Specific user group

Activate the
Travel Arranger

The activation focus with the biggest impact in B4B. Activating one TA can activate a whole company. This is also the starting point for our wider Travel Arranger platform investment.

15% of users · 82% of bookings 30% vs 11% activation rate 12 bookings/day from checklist experiment
Why this matters

Travel Arrangers have outsized impact — they don't just book for themselves, they organise booking for everyone else. They have different needs at first run (team setup, policy, oversight) and a different idea of what "success" looks like (managing a trip for someone else, not booking their own). Treating them as a separate activation group is the single best return on investment we can make.

Solo bookers are not a lost cause — they can recruit Travel Arrangers. When a solo user invites their TA, we turn our lowest-value user into our highest-value one.

04 Differentiation · Specific user group

Activate the Promoted User
on their timeline

Promoted users already show business travel intent and have higher CLV — but their average activation takes 44 days, so standard onboarding doesn't work for them. We measure them too early and act at the wrong time.

44-day avg activation window Already showing business intent Funnel-position signals at sign-up
Why this matters

Promoted Users are not all the same. A user reached at the top of the Bcom funnel (email, in-product prompt) is still exploring — they need to understand why B4B is worth switching to. A user reached at checkout confirmation has just completed a business booking and is ready to act now. Same flow, different intent — the experience should reflect that.

This is the area where the Bcom partnership matters most directly. We already know the acquisition channel at sign-up — we just don't use it.

06 · Pre-meeting prep

Questions Bcom will ask. Practise the answers.

Each of these will be asked in some form. How confidently we answer them shows how strong the framing is. Do not enter the meeting without a prepared answer to all six.

Where is the self-booker theme? You are ignoring the largest user count.
Self-bookers are covered by the Theme 1+2 foundation — universal sign-up, universal company landing. We chose not to personalise activation for them because their lifetime value does not justify the cost, and there is no intent signal strong enough to use for differentiation. Instead, they support Theme 3 — when solo users invite their TA, we turn our lowest-value user into our highest-value one.
Why is "Join Existing Company" not its own theme? It is a huge investment area.
JEC is what makes Theme 2 work. Separating it would mix mechanism with strategy — JEC is how Theme 2 happens, not a different area of focus. The engineering plan (Smart Matching, AID persistence at user level, SSO v3) all fits clearly under Theme 2. Pulling JEC out would create a fifth theme that looks like a feature.
You said "one flow" — so why two user-group-specific themes?
One flow at acquisition — that is the simplification. An adapted experience at activation — that is the personalisation. The sign-up flow stays unified (less complexity, faster delivery), but once a user is registered, the system uses what we already know about them (channel, role, behaviour) to shape their first experience. Same system, different output.
Travel Arrangers are only 15% of users — is that really enough to justify a dedicated theme?
They drive 82% of bookings. They activate at 30% vs 11% for solo-bookers — the cost per activation is already lower. And one activated TA can activate an entire company. The return on investment for TA activation is at least 5–10 times higher than for self-booker activation. It is the most impactful personalisation focus in B4B.
What is the single metric that ties this whole story together?
If we do not have one defined, this meeting is the wrong place to find out. Likely candidate: "% of new accounts reaching first booking within 30 days, segmented by channel and role." Decide before we walk in. Without it, the four themes look like four separate projects instead of one coherent strategy.
How does this connect to what Bcom invests in on their side?
Theme 4 (Promoted) is where the partnership matters most directly — Bcom controls the acquisition placement, B4B owns the activation experience. The funnel-position signal that drives our personalisation comes from Bcom. Theme 1 also depends on Bcom: property links, work-email detection, anonymous property previews all need Bcom-side touchpoints. We should present these as joint roadmap items, not B4B requests.
