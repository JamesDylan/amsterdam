01 · Strategy

Company Elevator Pitch

Multi-user companies are everything. Going from 1 to 2 users increases the likelihood of next booking by ~25% and reduces churn by ~20%. Companies with 100+ users generate 156 bookings/month vs 2.7 for singles. But 97% of our base has ≤5 users. Our job in Company Management is to make every B4B company bigger (more members) and better (more capable) — so the SMBs we have today book more, and the larger prospects we want tomorrow choose us.

Evidence from DSA company analysis 2025
02 · The structure

Two layers: Grow + Mature

We have four themes split across two distinct jobs B4B companies have to do. Two themes are about growing — more members per company. Two themes are about maturing — better setup, structure, and enterprise readiness.

Grow More members per company. Adding members reduces churn, increases booking volume and creates sticky customers.
01
Frictionless team growth
Make adding members the easiest thing an admin can do. Modernise the invite flow, capture name at invite, let Arrangers invite, share via Slack/Teams.
02
Unlock latent members
A huge pool of people already have a relationship with B4B but sit outside the team — guests at checkout, mismatched users, single-user accounts. Convert them.
→ once a company grows, mature how it works →
Mature Adding capabilities as companies grow. The features that let B4B serve SMBs better today and qualify for larger prospects tomorrow.
03
Accelerating the 'Aha' moment
Replace today's disconnected settings pages with a guided setup, real team and role structure, and a Manage Team experience that connects management to bookings.
04
Ready for larger companies
Prospects with 50+ travellers tell us hierarchy, SSO, legal entities and bulk onboarding are barriers to joining B4B. We need to qualify for them.

Frictionless team growth

Every barrier between an admin's intent to invite and a colleague joining should disappear. Today the invite flow is fragmented, clunky and doesn't capture the information needed to start booking immediately.

+25% next-booking likelihood (1→2 users) Each new profile = ~4-8 bookings Team Management R3 Estimate: +25 bookings/day
Why this matters

The invite flow is the the single biggest lever for company growth. Both at the onboarding stage and in future growth loops. The Team Management R3-5 PRD modernises the invite UX (R3), adds name capture so invitees can be booked for immediately (R4), and lets Travel Arrangers invite with admin approval — expanding who can grow the team beyond just admins.

A 10% increase in invites sent is estimated at +25 bookings/day for B4B. Small wins compound here.

02 Grow · More members Travel Arrangers: Strong

Unlock latent members

There is a hidden pool of people who already have a relationship with B4B but are not on a team — guests at checkout, users sitting in the wrong company, single-user accounts. Convert them.

83% of companies have guest travellers 86% of profiles in 2+ user companies are guests 9% of our bookings come from invited users 73%+ user interest in in-flow team expansion
Why this matters

The biggest latent pool is guest travellers. Colleagues added to a booking at checkout but never invited to the team. Release 5 of Team Management makes this a one-step flow: add a guest at checkout, offer to invite them to the team in the same moment. 73%+ of users said they want this.

The other latent pool is users who sit in the wrong company. Today the JEC mechanic catches some of this for new sign-ups, but not for existing companies or adds initiated by admins. Closing that gap converts more existing traffic into team members.

03 Mature · More capable Travel Arrangers: Strong

A company set up to succeed

Today's company setup is a collection of disconnected settings pages. Admins find each one independently with no guidance, no progress, and no sense of what a fully configured account looks like. We redesign around the outcomes admins and TAs actually care about.

Admin features embedded at key moments in the journey Manage Team = the TA's home screen Eos migration enables it
Why this matters

This is where the everyday admin and Travel Arranger experience lives. Three pieces of work converge here: Guided Setup (slide 13) replaces the fragmented settings pages with a step-by-step flow. Team and Role Foundations gives admins the ability to create teams, assign members, and limit Arranger visibility to their own team — the first time B4B will support a real org structure.

Manage Team UX experiments (the Admin Re-design PCB) test linking Manage Team with bookings — upcoming bookings per team member, link to make a booking, eventually a fully task-oriented redesign. Small experiments now, foundation for a larger admin UX redesign later.

All three depend on the Eos migration — referenced in the Tech Modernisation section (slides 20–22), but worth flagging here.

04 Mature · More capable Travel Arrangers: Limited

Ready for larger companies

Prospective customers with 50+ travellers tell us hierarchy, SSO, and enterprise-grade onboarding are barriers to joining B4B. We need to qualify for these companies before they sign — not promise it later.

100+ user co = 156 bookings/month vs 2.7 240k EU medium enterprises (50–249) 97% of base = ≤5 users today
Why this matters

The Team Assignment & Role Foundations PRD documents prospect interviews where lack of organisational hierarchy is named as a barrier to switching to B4B. This theme covers the features that are commodity at enterprise scale but missing from B4B today: SSO and Workday-style onboarding APIs, legal entity / VAT / tax capture at company level, modern approval workflows (Slack/Teams not just email), bulk traveller upload, modern policy guardrails across hotel/flight/car.

Most features here are admin-facing rather than Travel-Arranger-facing, which is why the TA relevance is limited. But hierarchy specifically benefits TAs in large orgs — limiting their visibility to their own team only.

08 · Pre-meeting prep

Questions Bcom will ask. Practise the answers.

Each of these will be asked in some form. How confidently we answer them shows how strong the framing is. Do not enter the meeting without a prepared answer to all six.

Where is the Travel Arranger theme? You said TAs are your most valuable user group.
TAs have their own dedicated investment section starting at slide 15 — that is where the holistic TA story lives. Inside Company Management, we have called out which themes serve TAs directly: T2 (Unlock latent members) is the TA's signature workflow (guest-at-checkout), T3 (Set up to succeed) is the TA's home screen (Manage Team experiments), T1 partially serves them (Arranger-can-invite), T4 less so. We chose not to create a separate TA theme here because it would compete with the dedicated TA section and split the message.
Why split T1 and T2 if they are both about growing the team?
Different mechanics. T1 is admin-led invite modernisation (Release 3 + 4 PRD): rebuild the form, capture name, expand who can invite. T2 is TA-led in-flow capture (Release 5 PRD): convert guests at checkout, catch users sitting in the wrong company. Different engineering paths, different metrics, different evidence base. Collapsing them buries the latent-member pool, which is where the biggest hidden growth sits — 83% of companies have guest travellers; 86% of profiles in 2+ user companies are guests.
Where is Insights & Analytics? Slide 14 has it as a theme.
Insights & Analytics is a real theme, but the scope and feature set is being developed separately and is not in this deck. Slide 14 is a placeholder for that section. Including it here without backing detail would weaken the messaging. We will cover it in its own slot when ready.
How does the Eos modernisation tie into this?
T3 and T4 are both load-bearing on Eos. Modern Company Management front end, Guided Setup, Team Assignment service, modern approval workflows, modern policy guardrails — these all require the modern stack. The Tech Modernisation section (slides 20–22) carries the technical narrative. In this section we reference Eos as the enabler without re-explaining it.
What is the single metric that ties this whole story together?
% of B4B companies with 2+ active users. It maps directly to the strategic objective, every PRD's evidence base, and the slide 2 strategy. Today: ~3% of our base is multi-user. Even modest movement on this number compounds heavily — going from 1 to 2 users alone is +25% next-booking likelihood and -20% churn.
How does this connect to what Bcom invests in on their side?
Two direct connections. (1) Identity/auth: SSO v3, Workday-style onboarding APIs, and domain enforcement (slide 11's "company quality gaps") all touch Bcom-side identity infrastructure. We need joint roadmap alignment here. (2) Data quality: the retroactive merging of duplicate companies and enforcing corporate domains over free email domains needs Bcom's input on the user-record side. These are joint problems, not B4B asks.
